## Detailed Case Studies For Top 3 Projects

### MeetMind

Overview:

Built a live meeting copilot designed to stay focused on real-time usefulness rather than generic summaries. The product records microphone audio, maintains a rolling transcript, presents exactly three live suggestions each refresh, and turns clicked suggestions into meeting-ready answers inside one persistent session.

What stands out:

- Suggestion selection is cue-aware, driven by unresolved questions, blockers, decisions, claims, dates, and terminology.
- Clicked answers are structured to help the user say, ask, verify, or explain something immediately.
- The user experience stays simple: transcript, live suggestions, and an answer workspace.

Why it matters:

Shows product judgement, applied AI workflow design, and the ability to turn model capabilities into a constrained experience with clear user interaction.

Links:

- Repo: https://github.com/VedantSawant6900/meetmind
- Demo: Unknown

### Reinforcement Learning Maze Solver

Overview:

Built a maze-solving reinforcement learning environment for comparing Deep Q-Networks and Q-Learning. The public repository supports configurable epsilon strategies, YAML-based experiment setup, saved training artifacts, and visualization output for reward, steps, epsilon decay, timing, and trajectory heatmaps.

What stands out:

- Side-by-side reinforcement learning comparison between DQN and Q-Learning
- Configurable exploration strategies including decay, Boltzmann, and performance-based epsilon
- YAML-driven experiments with saved results and visual analysis workflows

Why it matters:

Demonstrates reinforcement learning fundamentals, experiment design, configurable training workflows, and the ability to turn model runs into analyzable outputs.

Links:
